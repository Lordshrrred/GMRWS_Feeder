ASGM & Water

Why Artisanal Mining Cleanup Has To Include Water Security

When mercury pollution and water scarcity overlap, remediation has to protect both the contaminated site and the people living downstream.

Artisanal and small-scale gold mining regions often face two linked problems: mercury contamination and unreliable water systems. Treating those issues separately can leave communities exposed even after visible cleanup work begins.

Cleanup starts with water pathways

Mercury does not stay politely inside a tailings pile. Stormwater, stream channels, shallow wells, and flood events can spread contamination into places where families collect water or grow food.

Safe water changes community acceptance

Remediation programs need local trust. A project that also improves village water access can create a clearer public benefit and a stronger reason for communities to support long-term site controls.

Aquifer protection is a funding issue

Grantmakers and development banks increasingly want measurable social and environmental outcomes. A combined mercury and water strategy can speak to Minamata Convention goals, public health, climate adaptation, and rural infrastructure.
