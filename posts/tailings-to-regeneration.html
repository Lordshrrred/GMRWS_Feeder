Tailings Recovery

From Tailings Liability To Regeneration Asset

Tailings are often treated as a disposal problem. In the right operating model, they can become the starting point for cleanup, recovery, and land repair.

Mine tailings can contain residual mineral value, toxic exposure risk, unstable landforms, and long-term water contamination pathways. A practical regeneration model begins by seeing all four realities together.

Recover what can be recovered

Where mineral recovery is feasible, it can offset cleanup costs and reduce the amount of material requiring long-term management. The recovery plan should still be led by environmental controls, not only revenue potential.

Stabilize land and water

Tailings projects should address erosion, runoff, infiltration, and downstream sediment movement. Water protection is often the difference between a cleanup project and a temporary site improvement.

Plan the next land use early

Post-cleanup land does not regenerate by accident. Soil amendments, vegetation, hydrology, and future community or conservation uses need to be designed before equipment arrives.

Connect cleanup to measurable outcomes
